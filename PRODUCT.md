# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

ICT / Smart-Money retail traders and students who check the site before or during
a trading session. They arrive with chart context already in their head and want a
fast, credible read on each instrument's daily bias — the structural story behind
it (BOS/CHoCH, premium/discount, order blocks, FVGs, liquidity, draw-on-liquidity)
and how confident the rule-based engine is. A secondary audience is learners using
the daily output as worked examples of ICT concepts. Context of use: a quick
pre-session scan on desktop or phone, often returning daily to compare against
their own analysis and the community's vote.

## Product Purpose

A daily market-bias publication built on ICT concepts. Every trading day a
rule-based engine computes a bias for 12 instruments and the site publishes it as
an editorial "read of the day," surrounded by live extras (kill-zone tracker,
economic calendar) and per-instrument community voting + discussion. Success is a
trader trusting the read enough to return daily, and the analysis feeling like a
considered market note rather than an auto-generated dashboard. Educational only —
explicitly not financial advice.

## Brand Personality

Editorial broadsheet. Voice: composed, literate, quietly authoritative — the tone
of a printed financial almanac or a seasoned desk strategist's morning note. The
daily bias should read as a *verdict* a person would stand behind, not a metric a
machine spat out. Three words: considered, warm, precise. Emotional goal: earned
confidence and calm — never hype, urgency, or gamified dopamine.

## Anti-references

- **Over-minimal startup landing.** No sterile all-whitespace, single-accent,
  tiny-uppercase-tracked-eyebrow minimalism. The craft warmth (serif display, earthy
  signal tones, paper grain) is the product; stripping it to "clean SaaS" kills it.
- **Generic crypto / trading-app dashboard.** No neon-on-black, gradient glows,
  glassmorphism, or the big-number hero-metric template.
- **Busy retail-broker UI.** No MT4/TradingView toolbar density, clashing pure
  red/green, or alert-spam aesthetic.

## Design Principles

- **The bias is a verdict, not a metric.** Compose each instrument's read like an
  editorial judgment with supporting evidence, not a stat tile.
- **Earn trust through craft, not chrome.** Confidence comes from typography,
  rhythm, and precision of data — never from glow, gradients, or decoration.
- **Warmth is load-bearing.** The espresso/parchment palette, paper grain, and
  Fraunces serif are identity, not theme. Preserve them in every new surface.
- **Honest about uncertainty.** The engine can be wrong; confidence and outcomes are
  shown plainly. Never overstate certainty in copy or visual emphasis.
- **Dense but calm.** Information-rich (this audience wants the levels) without
  feeling cramped or noisy. Hierarchy does the work the user's eye shouldn't have to.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA: body text ≥4.5:1, large/bold text ≥3:1 against its surface in both
themes (the warm low-contrast palette makes this a real risk — verify, don't assume).
Never encode bias by color alone — always pair the bullish/bearish/neutral hue with
an icon or text label (colorblind safety, since red/green carries meaning here).
Full keyboard path through cards and the analysis overlay (focus-visible, Escape to
close, focus trap/restore on the dialog). Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for every
animation. Dual light/dark theme already serves ambient-light needs.
